28. What is the silo effect? Why does it exist? What problems does it create? How can an organization reduce or eliminate the silo effect?


Jawaban:

The Silo Effect refers to a lack of information flowing between groups or parts of an organization. This effect exists due to the lack of communication in individual departments in a company and departments

working on their own without considering the company as a whole or. As a result, the company does not work as one. It will cost more time and money due to the duplication of work, as in the departments are doing the same thing without knowing it. Tension will eventually rise among the different departments. And rumors will begin to spread. There will be delays in the operation, impacting both progress and the end result. To reduce the silo effect, a company can streamline procedures so that data is easily collected once rather than multiple times, Share best practices among facilities, departments and units, encourage feedback and involvement, have a facility “suggestion box”—and take the suggestions seriously, and listen to the resident and family councils.

b = Silo
c = Shipo

b = c - 5 <=> c = b + 5
a + b + c = 31

* a + b + c = 31
( 2b ) + b + ( b + 5 ) = 31
4b + 5 = 31
4b = 31 - 5
b = 26 / 4

Shipo = c = b + 5
= 26/4 + 5 = 26/4 + 20/4 = 46/4 = 11½ tahun
