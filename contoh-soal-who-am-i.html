Simple sentence : Kalimat yang terdiri dari subyek dan predikat, terdiri dari satu klausa induk.

Compound sentence: Kalimat yang terdiri dari dua klausa induk, dihubungkan dengan konjungsi.

Complex sentence: Kalimat yang terdiri dari satu klausa induk ditambah dengan satu/lebih klause anak, apabila anak kalimat diletakkan diawal diikuti oleh koma, dihubungkan konjungsi subordinatif.

Compound-complex sentence: Kalimat yang terdiri dari minimal dua klausa induk dan satu klausa anak.

Pembahasan

Simple sentence:

He (Subject) wore (Verb) a small golden earring on one ear (Object)His shirt (Subject) revealed  (Verb) a silver and turquoise necklace (Object)A small golden ring (Subject) pierced  (Verb) his left nostril (Object)On his feet (Adverb) were (Verb) chunky leather sandals (Subject).

Compound sentence:

I tell you the truth (clause 1), yet (conjunction) you don’t believe me (clause 2)His hair is twisted into thick dreadlocks (clause 1), and (conjunction) it fell almost to his shoulders (clause 2)He wore a faded pair of jeans (clause 1) , and (conjunction)  it flared into a wide bell over his ankles  (clause 2) I like my job and boss (clause 1), but (conjunction) I’m  not paid enough (clause 2)

Complex sentence:

The children started talking (independent clause) when (subordinating conjunction) the teacher went out (dependent clause)Rudi and Roni get a scholarship (independent clause) because (subordinating conjunction) they are so smart (dependent clause)Unless (subordinating conjunction) the meeting is cancelled (dependent clause), I will be sitting inside the meeting room (independent clause), for (subordinating conjunction) I am being asked to take note for the minutes of meeting (dependent clause)The singer looked (independent clause) as if (subordinating conjunction) he had just stepped out of the 1960s (dependent clause)Over his shirt, he wore a soft leather vest (independent clause) that (subordinating conjunction) ended at his waist in a beaded fringe (dependent clause)While (subordinating conjunction) he was cleaning the kitchen (dependent clause), he found an oil spill (independent clause)

Compound-complex sentence:

The puzzle is too complicated (independent clause), but (conjunction) I would like to solve it (independent clause) before somebody else did (dependent clause)  I feel that I am not a possessive girlfriend, but my boyfriend thinks so.  The dog ran off when I chased him, but I didn't carePeople who do exercise regularly rarely get sick, and they usually have a good immune systemTanti loves her brother, and he loves her too because she pays his bills You will never know what she is thinking about, yet you can ask her
